Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are these calculations?

They use the official 2025–2026 tax brackets and social security rates from each country's tax authority. They are accurate for a single person with no dependents and standard deductions. Regional taxes (e.g. Germany's church tax, Italy's regional levy) are not included. Always verify with a local tax professional before making financial decisions.

Where do the currency rates come from?

Live exchange rates are fetched from the Frankfurter API (api.frankfurter.app) on every page load. This is a free, open-source API sourced from the European Central Bank. Rates update every business day.

What's the difference between Employee and Self-Employed?

As an employee, your employer also pays social security on top of your salary. As self-employed, you pay both the employee and employer shares of social security yourself, which is typically 30–40% more in total contributions.

Is my data saved anywhere?

No. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. Saved comparisons are stored in your browser's localStorage only — they never leave your device. No analytics, no tracking, no server.

Which countries are included?

Germany, Netherlands, France, Spain, Italy, Poland, Portugal, Sweden, Denmark, Austria, Belgium, Ireland, Czechia, Hungary, Romania, Greece, Finland, Luxembourg, Switzerland (non-EU but popular), and Norway (non-EU but Schengen). 20 countries total.

Why is Hungary and Romania net salary so high?

Both countries have extremely low flat income tax rates (Hungary 15%, Romania 10%). However, social security contributions are high (Romania 35%), and average salaries are much lower than Western Europe, so absolute take-home amounts are lower even if the percentage kept is higher.
